# Mission: Solve a Rubik's Cube

## Why
Matt wants to be able to take a scrambled 3×3 Rubik's cube and solve it, unaided, at least once. The goal is the achievement itself — not speed, not theory.

## Success looks like
- Take any scrambled cube and solve it from start to finish with no guide, notes, or app
- Explain (at a high level) what each stage of the solve is doing, not just parrot moves

## Constraints
- Owns a cube already; total beginner — cannot solve any part of it yet
- Learns in weekend deep-dive sessions (occasional, long), not daily drills
- Memorisation load should be kept minimal — pick the beginner method with the fewest algorithms

## Out of scope
- Other puzzles (2×2, 4×4, etc.)
- Group theory / mathematical foundations
- Competition-style speedcubing for its own sake (timing obsession, finger-trick drilling, full 57-OLL / 21-PLL memorisation)

## Extension — opened 2026-06-09
The original goal (an unaided solve) is effectively banked. Matt chose to extend the mission: he now wants to genuinely solve *better*, and has explicitly accepted that this costs new algorithms. So the previous hard "no new algorithms / fewest possible" constraint is relaxed — but only deliberately, the gentlest high-leverage way, keeping memorisation as low as each upgrade allows.

- **Now in scope:** the intermediate last layer — 4-look / 2-look OLL & PLL — replacing the beginner method's clunky last-layer stages. Entry point: 2-look OLL, orienting corners with a single repeated algorithm (Sune), which retires the slow `R' D' R D` grind.
- **Still out:** chasing a stopwatch and finger tricks as ends in themselves. The aim is a smoother, fewer-move, more elegant solve — not a competition time.
- F2L remains a *maybe later* — only if Matt asks after the last layer is upgraded.

### ✅ Banked 2026-06-17 (record 0013)
The intermediate last-layer extension is **achieved**. Matt reports all algorithms now "in my hands," including both PLLs (U-perm + A-perm). His everyday solve is now: cross → corner inserts → second layer → **OLL** (yellow cross, then Sune) → **PLL** (A-perm corners, then U-perm edges). The beginner align-cross / position-corners / orient-corners grind (`R' D' R D`) is retired; the solve card has been swapped to the intermediate last layer. First layer also smoothed to read-the-case corner inserts (record 0012). The whole "smoother, fewer-move solve" thread is now delivered.

**Next fork (Matt's choice, not pushed):** look-ahead / pause-trimming on the solve he has, **or** F2L (pairing the first-layer corner with its middle edge — the big remaining inefficiency). Speedcubing-for-the-stopwatch stays out of scope.
