Rubik's Cube · Reference

Glossary

Terminology for the beginner (layer-by-layer) method. Only terms Matt has demonstrated understanding of belong here.

Anatomy

Center
The single fixed sticker in the middle of each face. Centers never move relative to each other — they define what colour each face must become.
Edge
A piece with two stickers, sitting between two centers. There are 12.
Corner
A piece with three stickers. There are 8.
Avoid: "corner sticker" when the whole piece is meant — solve pieces, not stickers.
Layer
A horizontal third of the cube. The first layer is the white face plus its ring of side colours; the second is the middle band of edges; the last is the yellow layer.

Notation

Face letters F R U L D B
Front, Right, Up, Left, Down, Back. A bare letter means turn that face a quarter-turn clockwise, as if looking at that face head-on.
Prime '
A quarter-turn counterclockwise. R' undoes R.
The trigger R U R' U'
The four-move sequence used to insert all four white corners by repetition (1, 3, or 5 times). Disturbs the bottom layer temporarily but restores it each cycle.
Avoid: "sexy move" in workspace files — same thing, but "trigger" is our canonical name.

Technique

Solving grip
White face down, yellow center up, held that way for everything after the cross. The bottom is the trophy shelf; the top is the workbench.
Daisy
The staging pattern for the white cross — four white edge stickers around the yellow center, then each "petal" is matched to its center and dropped down with a 180° face turn.
Parking
Turning the top layer with U moves until a piece sits where an algorithm can act on it: a corner directly above its home slot, or an edge forming the upside-down T over its matching center.
