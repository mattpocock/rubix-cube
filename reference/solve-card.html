Rubik's Cube · Reference

Solve Card

The full solve in order, one stage per box. The intermediate solve — read-the-case first layer, then 4-look last layer (orient, then permute).

1. White cross — the daisy

Goal: four white edges around the white center, each side colour matching its center. No algorithms — intuition only.

  1. Daisy: yellow center up. Bring all four white edge stickers around the yellow center (white petals on a yellow flower). Turn the top first if needed so you don't knock out a placed petal.
  2. Match: turn the top (U moves only) until a petal's side colour sits directly above the matching center.
  3. Drop: turn that face 180° (e.g. F2). Repeat for all four petals.

Done when: flipped over, white cross with all four side colours matching their centers. Half a cross is no cross.

2. White corners — read & insert

Goal: first layer complete. Grip: white down, yellow up — the solving grip from here on. Park each corner above its empty slot, then read where its white sticker faces and fire one insert.

White sticker faces…Insert
Right faceR U R'
Front face (mirror)F' U' F
Up (white on top)R U2 R' U' R U R'
  • Park first: U moves until the corner sits directly above its home slot (its two side colours match the two centers below), held at top-front-right.
  • White-on-top is the long one — its setup R U2 R' just kicks white onto a side, then it's the basic insert. (Mirror: F' U2 F U F' U' F.)
  • Read the case and fire once — don't grind the same move 1/3/5 times.
Rescue — corner stuck in the bottom layer (wrong slot or twisted): hold that slot at front-right, one R U R' pops it into the top layer, then read and insert normally.

Done when: solid white bottom and a solid bottom row ring on all four sides. The ring is the real test, not the white face.

3. Second layer — the two inserts

Goal: four middle edges in. Hunt the top layer for edges with no yellow sticker.

  1. Park: U moves until the edge's outward sticker matches the center below it — the upside-down T. Rotate the cube so that face is front.
  2. Pick the insert by where the top sticker's colour lives:
Top sticker matches…Algorithm
Right center → right insertU R U' R' U' F' U F
Left center → left insert (mirror)U' L' U L U F U' F'
  • Two four-move chunks, each with the trigger's do-undo rhythm. Say it as two breaths.
  • First move steers the edge away from its slot — trust it.
  • Mid-algorithm the first layer looks broken. It isn't — finish all eight moves.
  • F = front face, clockwise as you look straight at it (top row swings right). A reversed F is the #1 way this "destroys" the first layer.
Rescue — edge stuck in the middle layer (wrong slot or flipped): hold its slot at front-right, do the right insert with whatever top edge lands there. The stuck edge pops up; solve it normally.

Done when: four solid two-row walls, only the yellow layer scrambled. Two-thirds solved.

4. Yellow cross — the F-sandwich

Goal: four yellow edge stickers facing up (a yellow cross on top). Ignore corners; ignore side colours. Read the pattern, don't hunt pieces.

F R U R' U' F'

The trigger wrapped in an F-sandwich: F — trigger — F-prime.

Top-face shapeHold it…Apply
Dot (no edges up)any way3×
L-shape (two adjacent)L points back-left (like a 9)2×
Line (two opposite)horizontal, left–right1×
  • The chain is dot → L → line → cross. Re-read and re-orient between applications — don't fire blindly from one grip.
  • Side colours of the cross edges don't matter yet — that's stage 5.
Rescue — exactly 1 or 3 yellow edges up: impossible on a healthy cube. A piece is physically flipped; pop it out and reseat it. No algorithm fixes this.

Done when: yellow cross on top, two-row walls still intact below.

5. Yellow corners — the Sune (OLL)

Goal: the whole top face yellow. Don't care where the side colours sit yet — just get every corner showing yellow up. Same fixed grip; R and U only.

R U R' U R U2 R'

Count how many corners already show yellow on top — always 0, 1, or 2 — then aim and fire. Re-count and re-aim between every go.

Corners yellow-upHold so that…
1that corner sits at top-front-left, then Sune.
0 or 2the top-front-left corner has its yellow facing left, then Sune.
  • The loop: count → hold → Sune → re-count, re-hold → fire again. Worst case three Sunes.
  • A half-yellow top mid-stage is normal. The only real mistake is firing again without re-aiming.

Done when: a solid yellow top face. The ring below is still scrambled — the last two stages slide it home.

6. Place the corners — headlights (PLL)

Goal: all four top corners in their home corner, top still yellow. Recognise by headlights — two top corners on a face showing the same colour as each other.

R' F R' B2 R F' R' B2 R2

A 3-cycle — places three corners; the front-left one is the anchor and stays. Walk the four sides; headlight faces are always 0, 1, or 4, never 2 or 3.

Done when: every top corner's two side stickers match their faces — all four corners home. Fire at most twice.

7. Cycle the edges — the finish (PLL)

Goal: a solved cube. Only the four top edges remain. The anchor is a solved edge — a top edge whose front colour already matches the center below it.

R U' R U R U R U' R' U' R2

A 3-cycle of edges, R and U only; the back edge is the anchor and stays put.

You seeDo
One solved edgehold it at the back, run once. Didn't solve? The cycle ran the other way — run once more.
No solved edgerun once from any angle → a solved edge appears → re-aim to the back, run again.

Done when: six flat faces. The cube is solved.
