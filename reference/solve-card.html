Rubik's Cube · Reference

Solve Card

The full solve in order, one stage per box. Complete — scramble to solved, all seven stages.

1. White cross — the daisy

Goal: four white edges around the white center, each side colour matching its center. No algorithms — intuition only.

  1. Daisy: yellow center up. Bring all four white edge stickers around the yellow center (white petals on a yellow flower). Turn the top first if needed so you don't knock out a placed petal.
  2. Match: turn the top (U moves only) until a petal's side colour sits directly above the matching center.
  3. Drop: turn that face 180° (e.g. F2). Repeat for all four petals.

Done when: flipped over, white cross with all four side colours matching their centers. Half a cross is no cross.

2. White corners — the trigger

Goal: first layer complete. Grip: white down, yellow up — the solving grip from here on.

R U R' U'
  1. Find a white corner in the top layer.
  2. Park it (U moves) directly above its home slot — its two non-white colours match the two centers below.
  3. Hold it at top-front-right, home slot beneath it.
  4. Trigger until it drops in white-side-down: 1, 3, or 5 repeats. Check after each repeat; don't rotate the cube between repeats.
Rescue — corner stuck in the bottom layer (wrong slot or twisted): hold that slot at front-right, one trigger pops it into the top layer, then solve normally.

Done when: solid white bottom and a solid bottom row ring on all four sides. The ring is the real test, not the white face.

3. Second layer — the two inserts

Goal: four middle edges in. Hunt the top layer for edges with no yellow sticker.

  1. Park: U moves until the edge's outward sticker matches the center below it — the upside-down T. Rotate the cube so that face is front.
  2. Pick the insert by where the top sticker's colour lives:
Top sticker matches…Algorithm
Right center → right insertU R U' R' U' F' U F
Left center → left insert (mirror)U' L' U L U F U' F'
  • Two four-move chunks, each with the trigger's do-undo rhythm. Say it as two breaths.
  • First move steers the edge away from its slot — trust it.
  • Mid-algorithm the first layer looks broken. It isn't — finish all eight moves.
  • F = front face, clockwise as you look straight at it (top row swings right). A reversed F is the #1 way this "destroys" the first layer.
Rescue — edge stuck in the middle layer (wrong slot or flipped): hold its slot at front-right, do the right insert with whatever top edge lands there. The stuck edge pops up; solve it normally.

Done when: four solid two-row walls, only the yellow layer scrambled. Two-thirds solved.

4. Yellow cross — the F-sandwich

Goal: four yellow edge stickers facing up (a yellow cross on top). Ignore corners; ignore side colours. Read the pattern, don't hunt pieces.

F R U R' U' F'

The trigger wrapped in an F-sandwich: F — trigger — F-prime.

Top-face shapeHold it…Apply
Dot (no edges up)any way3×
L-shape (two adjacent)L points back-left (like a 9)2×
Line (two opposite)horizontal, left–right1×
  • The chain is dot → L → line → cross. Re-read and re-orient between applications — don't fire blindly from one grip.
  • Side colours of the cross edges don't matter yet — that's stage 5.
Rescue — exactly 1 or 3 yellow edges up: impossible on a healthy cube. A piece is physically flipped; pop it out and reseat it. No algorithm fixes this.

Done when: yellow cross on top, two-row walls still intact below.

5. Align the cross — the edge swap

Goal: each cross edge sitting above its matching center. Only R and U turns — no F to get wrong.

R U R' U R U2 R' U

Swaps the front and left cross edges; back and right stay put. Shuffles unsolved corners — that's free.

  1. Spin the top (U clicks) to the position with the most matches — always 2 or 4, never 1 or 3.
  2. Four match → done.
  3. Two adjacent match → rotate the cube so the matched pair are at back and right, run the algorithm once.
  4. Two opposite match → run it once from anywhere to break into the adjacent case, then set up and run again.
  5. Finish with a top spin to line everything up.

Done when: four T-shapes — two solid rows plus matching cross edge on every side. Only the four yellow corners remain.

6. Position the corners — the merry-go-round

Goal: every yellow corner in its home slot, twist irrelevant. A corner is "correct" if its three colours match the three faces it touches — any way up.

U R U' L' U R' U' L

Cycles three corners; the top-front-right corner stays put. Right hand, left hand, alternating top–side.

  1. Audit all four corners by colour-set. Count correct: always 0, 1, or 4 — never 2 or 3.
  2. Four → done. One → rotate the whole cube (not U!) so it's at top-front-right; run the algorithm; if needed run it once more, same grip.
  3. Zero → run once from any grip to create a correct corner, then handle as the one-correct case.

Done when: all four colour-set audits pass. May still look messy — twisted corners are next stage's job. Trust the audit, not the aesthetics.

7. Orient the corners — the final twist

Goal: a solved cube. Twist each corner in place until its yellow faces up. New letter: D = bottom layer, front row slides left (D' slides it right).

R' D' R D

Twists whatever sits at top-front-right. Repeat in pairs — each corner takes 2 or 4 repetitions.

  1. Hold a twisted corner at top-front-right.
  2. Run the four moves twice. Yellow up? Done. No? Twice more — always pairs, never odd.
  3. Turn U only (never the whole cube, never the bottom layers) to bring the next twisted corner to top-front-right. Repeat.
  4. Finish with U turns to line up the top layer.
Keep going — mid-step the bottom layers look destroyed. That's the algorithm borrowing pieces; it repays everything as the last corner finishes. The only real mistakes are stopping early, rotating the cube, or moving the bottom layers between corners.

Done when: six flat faces. The cube is solved.
