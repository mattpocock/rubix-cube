Rubik's Cube · Lesson 2

White Corners: One Trigger, Whole First Layer

You already know the only algorithm this lesson needs. By the end, one third of the cube is solved.

Start by building your white cross (good practice — the daisy, then drop the petals). Done? Now learn exactly one new thing: how to use R U R' U' — the sexy move you drilled in Lesson 1 — to insert all four white corners.

1. Flip the cube: white goes on the bottom

In Lesson 1 you finished with the white cross facing up so you could admire it. Now turn the cube over — white cross on the bottom, yellow center on top — and keep it that way. This is the solving grip for the entire rest of the solve, today and in every future lesson.

Why hide the part you solved? Because all the unsolved pieces you need to work with are now in the top layer, where you can see them and move them freely with U turns — without breaking the cross. From here on, the bottom is the trophy shelf and the top is the workbench.

2. Every corner has exactly one home

A white corner piece has three stickers: white plus two colours. Those two colours tell you its home: the white-red-green corner belongs in the bottom layer between the red and green centers, white facing down. Pieces, not stickers — there is one slot it fits, and no other.

Check yourself

You spot a corner with white, orange and blue stickers in the top layer. Where is its home?

3. The whole technique

  1. Find a white corner in the top layer. (If all the loose white corners are stuck in the bottom — see the rescue move below.)
  2. Park it above its home. Turn the top layer (U moves only) until the corner sits directly above its slot — i.e. its two non-white colours match the two centers below it.
  3. Hold it at front-right. Rotate the whole cube (no face turns) so that corner is at the top-front-right, with its home slot at the bottom-front-right directly beneath it.
  4. Repeat R U R' U' until it drops in solved. Do the four moves, look at the corner. Not solved? Do them again. It will land white-side-down in 1, 3, or 5 repeats depending on how it was twisted. Don't rotate the cube between repeats.
  5. Next corner. Repeat from step 1 until all four are in. First layer complete.
R U R' U'
repeat 1, 3, or 5 times — checking the corner after each repeat
Why repeating it works: each round of R U R' U' takes the corner out of the slot, twists it, and puts it back — while everything it disturbs in the bottom layer gets restored by the end of each round. So you can safely keep cycling until the corner shows up white-side-down. You proved this in Lesson 1: six repeats return the whole cube to where it started. Solving a corner just means stopping at the right point in that cycle.

Check yourself

You've parked a corner above its slot and done R U R' U' twice. The corner is in the slot but the white sticker faces front, not down. What now?

The rescue move

What if a white corner is already in the bottom layer but in the wrong slot, or in the right slot twisted the wrong way? You can't park it above anything — it's not in the top layer.

Easy: hold the cube so that corner is at the bottom-front-right, then do one round of R U R' U'. That pops it up into the top layer. Now it's a normal case — park it above its real home and repeat the trigger as usual.

Check yourself

The white-red-blue corner is sitting in the slot between the green and orange centers. What's the full plan?

4. What done looks like

Bottom face: solid white
Each side: a solid bottom row matching its center — a full ring around the cube
The real test is the ring, not the white face. A solid white face with a broken ring of side colours means a corner is in the wrong slot (stickers, not pieces!). Check all four sides: bottom row solid, matching the center. That's a genuinely solved first layer — one third of the cube.

Practice — do this before the next session

1. Full scramble → cross → all four corners. Check the ring on all four sides.

2. Repeat until you've completed the first layer three times in a row without help. (Cross included — this also finishes off your Lesson 1 fluency target.)

3. While repeating the trigger, say it out loud — "R, U, R prime, U prime" — until your hands do it without thinking. You'll be using this exact trigger again in the later stages.
