Rubik's Cube · Lesson 7 · The Last One

The Final Twist: Orient the Corners

Every piece is home. Some corners just sit twisted. Four moves, repeated patiently, finish the cube — and your first full solve.

The grip does not change. Yellow on top, white down, F at your chest — one last time. From positioned corners, the only thing left is twisting some corners in place so their yellow stickers face up. This lesson's algorithm does exactly that, and nothing else survives it being wrong — so it comes with the solve's biggest warning, which we'll get to.

1. One new letter: D

This algorithm uses the bottom layer for the first time. D is the Down face — clockwise as you look at it from below, which means the front-bottom row slides to your left. D' slides it right. Same sanity ritual as every face: D then D' should change nothing (Ruwix notation).

2. The algorithm — four moves, repeated

R' D' R D
Twists the TOP-FRONT-RIGHT corner · repeat the four moves until its yellow faces up

That's it — the shortest algorithm of the method (Ruwix, step 7). It works on one corner at a time: whatever sits at top-front-right gets twisted one-third of a turn for every two repetitions. So each corner needs the four moves either twice or four times — after every second repetition, glance at the top-front-right sticker: yellow up means that corner is done.

The cube will look destroyed. Keep going. Mid-procedure, your finished white layer and second layer will look mangled. This is the step where, per Ruwix, "most people get lost" — they panic and stop halfway. Don't. The damage is a loan: the algorithm borrows pieces from the bottom layers and pays every one back by the time all the corners are oriented. The only way to actually break the cube here is to stop early or move the bottom two layers between corners.

3. The procedure

  • Hold a twisted corner at top-front-right. Get it there by turning U only — for the first corner, turning the whole cube is still fine, but once you've started the step, U turns only.
  • Run R' D' R D twice. Yellow on top at front-right? Done with this corner. Not yet? Run it twice more — never once more, always in pairs.
  • Turn U (just the top layer — not the whole cube, and never the bottom layers) to bring the next twisted corner to top-front-right. Ignore how broken everything below looks.
  • Repeat until every yellow sticker faces up. The bottom two layers snap back to solved exactly as the last corner finishes.
  • Final U turns: spin the top layer until its sides match. Look at the cube. It's solved.
Why U turns this time, when Lesson 6 forbade them? In Lesson 6 the cross was aligned and U turns would un-align it — so you rotated the whole cube instead. Here it's reversed: the algorithm's "loan" lives in the bottom two layers, anchored to your fixed grip. Rotate the whole cube and the loan never gets repaid. U turns are safe because the whole top layer gets re-aligned in one go at the very end. Each stage protects whatever is solved — and right now, the fragile part is below, not above.

4. What done looks like

This is the only stage with no consolation prize for "done": done is a solved cube. Six flat faces. And you got there from a genuine scramble, stage by stage, knowing what each one did:

White cross → white corners → second layer → yellow cross → align the cross → position the corners → the final twist. That chain is your whole method, and it's on one page: the solve card.

Practice — the one that counts

1. Warm up the twist on a solved cube: run R' D' R D twice — the front-right corner twists and the cube looks hurt. Run it four more times — everything heals. That's the loan being repaid, in miniature.

2. Scramble the cube properly — a couple of dozen random moves, no peeking at patterns.

3. Solve it, start to finish. Solve card open is allowed — the mission was never blind memorisation, it was solving with understanding. When the last face clicks flat: that's the mission, complete. Come tell me.

There is no Lesson 8. There's just a scrambled cube and you.
