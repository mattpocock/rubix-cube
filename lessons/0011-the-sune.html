Rubik's Cube · Lesson 11 · Optimising — the real upgrade

The Sune

You've decided to actually get better, new algorithms and all. So here's the first real upgrade: retire the R' D' R D grind and paint the whole yellow top with one algorithm.

This is a turning point, so let's be clear about what's changing. Your beginner last layer has four stages — make the cross, align it, position the corners, then twist them upright with repeated R' D' R D. That last stage is the slowest, fiddliest thing you do: a long grind that destroys the bottom and slowly repays it, one corner at a time.

The intermediate world does the last layer in a different, faster shape called 4-look last layer: first orient everything (get the whole top yellow), then permute everything (slide the pieces home). The first half — getting the top fully yellow — is called OLL (Orientation of the Last Layer). [TactileCuber]

This lesson teaches the corner half of OLL — with a single algorithm. You already make the yellow cross (F R U R' U' F', lesson 4). Add one more algorithm — the Sune — applied one to three times, and the entire top face goes yellow. That one algorithm replaces your whole stage-7 grind.

1. Meet the Sune

The Sune is the most famous algorithm in cubing, and it's barely a stranger — it opens with the same R U R' motion buried inside the trigger you've used since lesson 2.

New algorithm — the Sune
R U R' U R U2 R'
"Right-up-right… right-up-up-right." Seven moves, only the R and U faces — no F, nothing to get backwards.

One Sune lifts three corners, spins them, and sets them back. Run it from the right starting position and, after one to three goes, every corner shows yellow on top. Notice the relief: no F, no D, no whole-cube rotations — the two faces you trust most, and nothing else. [Speedsolving Wiki]

2. Where to hold it — the whole trick

The Sune only works if you aim it correctly first. Before each go, glance at the top and count how many corners already show yellow on top. It's always 0, 1, or 2 (never 3; four means you're done). That count tells you exactly how to hold the cube: [speedcube.us]

You seeHold it so that…
1 yellow on top…that single already-correct corner sits at top-front-left. Then Sune.
0 or 2 on top…the top-front-left corner has its yellow sticker facing left. Then Sune.
The loop: count → hold by the rule → Sune → re-count and re-hold → Sune again if needed. Re-aiming between goes is the part beginners skip — don't. Worst case is three Sunes; most cases are one or two. When the top is solid yellow, you're done.

And there's a free self-check: if the top isn't fully yellow after a Sune, that's fine and expected — just re-count, re-hold, and fire again. The only real mistake is running a second Sune without re-aiming. Trust the rule, not the look of the half-finished top.

3. Drill the Sune into your hands

Same trainer you know. Tap the seven moves in order — green locks them in, a wrong tap resets the run. Start Guided, then go Blind and rebuild it from memory. Goal: five blind runs in a row, saying each move aloud so your hand learns the rhythm.

4. Take it to the cube

  • Solve up to the yellow cross. Scramble, then run your normal solve only as far as stage 4 — a yellow cross on top, corners still random. Stop there.
  • Count, hold, Sune. Count the yellow-on-top corners (0/1/2), hold by the rule above, and run the Sune. Re-count, re-hold, repeat. Watch the top fill in until it's a solid yellow face.
  • Just drill the orientation. Don't worry that the sides are scrambled — getting the full yellow top is the win for today. Reach it ten times from ten different crosses, and the count-hold-Sune loop becomes automatic.
  • Feel the upgrade. Compare it to the old R' D' R D stage. Fewer moves, one familiar algorithm, no cube-flipping. That's what "optimising" actually buys you.
Keep your old method for now. A solid yellow top isn't a solved cube — the last-layer pieces still need sliding home, and that's the permutation half (PLL), coming in lesson 12. Until then, build the Sune in parallel: keep solving full cubes the beginner way, but practise OLL on the side. Once you've got both halves, we swap the whole last layer over and you'll have a genuinely faster solve.

Practice — this session & after

Now: trainer to five blind runs, then on the cube reach a solid yellow top from ten different yellow crosses using count-hold-Sune.

Between sessions: after each normal solve, do a few extra OLL-only reps — cross, then Sune to full yellow. Say the count and the hold aloud ("two up, yellow-left, Sune").
