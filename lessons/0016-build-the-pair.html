Build the Pair

You couldn't find ready-made pairs to slot in — and you were right: they almost never appear on a scramble. The real skill isn't finding pairs. It's building them — and now you'll see exactly how.

That frustration last session was the lesson working, not failing. A corner already joined to its edge, white politely facing the side, is rare. The other three slots you have to make. Good news: building a pair is just two small skills, and the insert at the end is still the move you already own.

1. How to read these diagrams

Every picture below is your cube held in the normal solving grip — yellow on top, green in front, red on the right, looking down at the front-right corner where you build. The whole method in one line. Get the corner and its edge both up in the top layer, then scoop them together and drop them in one motion. That scoop is a single short sequence — chosen by where the corner's white sticker points. [Ruwix]

2. First, get both pieces up top

You can only build a pair on your workbench — the top layer. Often one piece is stuck in the slot wrong (corner twisted, or edge flipped). Pop it out the way you already know:

Pop a stuck piece into the top layer
R U R'
Hold the bad slot at front-right and fire once — the jammed piece lifts into the top layer. Now both pieces are up where you can pair them.

3. Then scoop — read the white sticker

Park the corner at the top-front-right and look at where its white sticker points. That one read picks your scoop. First bring the edge (blue) to the spot shown with U turns, then fire:

Case 1 · white on a side
White faces the FRONT, edge up at top-right.
U R U' R'
The U sets the edge over the slot; the trigger snaps the pair and drops it in.
Case 2 · the mirror
White faces the RIGHT, edge up at top-front.
U' F' U F
Same idea flipped to the front face — the exact mirror of Case 1.
Case 3 · the stubborn one
White points UP.
R U2 R' U' R U R'
The first three moves (R U2 R') roll white off the top onto a side — then it's just a side case again.

Every sequence here was checked on a cube simulator: each disturbs only the front-right slot and the top layer — never the rest of your first two layers. [verification]

Two cases are one idea; the third reduces to them. Cases 1 and 2 are mirror twins — white on a side, scoop with a trigger. Case 3 just pays three extra moves to knock white down first. So you're really learning one move and its mirror, plus "if white's on top, knock it down."

4. Drill the read

The moves are easy; the skill is the read. A cube appears — say where white points, pick the scoop. Guided names it; Blind makes you call it from the picture. Goal: five in a row.

5. Take it to the cube

  • White cross, then pick any slot. Rotate the whole cube so its empty slot is front-right.
  • Get both pieces up top. Find the white corner for that slot and its edge; pop out whatever's stuck with R U R'.
  • Read white, bring the edge, scoop. Match the picture: white on a side → the trigger twin; white on top → knock it down first. The pair drops home.
  • Repeat for all four slots. Now you're building pairs, not waiting for them — the thing that was missing last session.
Still in parallel — for one more week. Keep finishing any slot that fights you the old stage-2/3 way; you lose nothing. But you should now be able to build most pairs deliberately. When all four slots feel buildable, tell me — that's when we retire stages 2 and 3 on your solve card and fold F2L in for good. One case still waits after that: when the corner and edge show the same colour on top and refuse to pair (the "hide-and-seek" case) — we'll take that last.

Practice — this session & after

Now: trainer to five blind reads, then on the cube build and insert at least one pair into each of the four slots across a few scrambles.

Between sessions: after each solve, rebuild one slot from scratch — pop both pieces up, then scoop — saying the read aloud ("white on front, edge to the right, U R U' R'").
