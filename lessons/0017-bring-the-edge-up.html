Bring the Edge Up

Last lesson assumed both pieces were already up top, ready to scoop. But the edge is often jammed down in a slot — so the question you hit is exactly the right one: how do you get it up onto the workbench?

You've spotted the real gap. Lesson 16 showed you how to scoop a pair together — but every diagram had the edge already sitting up in the top layer. On a real scramble it often isn't. The edge that belongs in your slot is frequently wedged in the middle row of the cube, in the right slot facing the wrong way, or down in a slot it doesn't belong to. Before you can pair it, you have to lift it out onto the workbench — the top layer. That's this lesson, and it's one move you already own.

1. An edge has only two homes

When you're about to build a pair, the edge you need is in exactly one of two places. Learning to tell them apart in a glance is the whole skill:

Up top — ready

Pair it now.

The edge sits in the top layer. You can bring it beside the corner and scoop straight away.

Trapped in a slot

Evict it first.

The edge is wedged in the middle row (here, blue stuck in the front-right slot). You can't pair it from down there — lift it up first.

The workbench rule. You can only build a pair in the top layer. So step zero of every slot is: is my edge up top, or trapped in the middle? If it's up top, you're already on Lesson 16. If it's trapped, you do one thing first — bring it up.

2. The move that frees it — you already know it

Here's the good part. The move that lifts a trapped edge onto the workbench is the same R U R' you used to pop a stuck corner last lesson. Hold the slot the edge is stuck in at the front-right (rotate the whole cube to bring it there, exactly as always), and fire once:

Evict a trapped edge into the top layer
R U R'
The R lifts the front-right slot up the right-hand side; U carries it away across the top; R' closes the slot back up — leaving the edge sitting in the top layer, your white cross untouched. Now it's a Lesson-16 case.
One move clears the slot — you don't have to be surgical. If the corner is jammed in there too, the very same R U R' lifts both pieces out to the top at once. So you never need a special "edge only" trick: put the messy slot at front-right, fire R U R', and whatever was stuck is now up on the workbench where you can pair it cleanly. [J Perm]

Checked on the cube simulator: with any version of the edge stuck in the front-right slot — facing the wrong way, or sharing the slot with the corner — a single R U R' sends it up to the top layer and leaves the white cross fully intact. [verification]

What about an edge in the right slot but twisted the wrong way? Same idea — it's just trapped in a slot, so the same R U R' frees it. But that case (and twisted corners, and wrong pieces) has its own tells worth learning to read, so it gets its own lesson: Lesson 18 — Fix a Wrong Slot.

3. The whole loop, start to finish

Put it together and every slot is the same three-beat rhythm. Two of the three beats are pure Lesson 16 — the only new beat is the first:

  • Check, then free. Edge up top already? Skip ahead. Trapped in a slot? Rotate that slot to front-right and fire R U R' — now it's up.
  • Read & scoop. Park the corner at top-front-right, read where its white sticker points, bring the edge alongside with U turns, and scoop: U R U' R' / U' F' U F / (white-up) R U2 R' U' R U R'.
  • Done — next slot. The pair drops home. Rotate to the next slot and start the rhythm again.

4. Drill the first beat

The reading is the skill, not the move. A cube appears — decide in a glance: is the edge already up top (go pair it), or is it trapped (evict with R U R' first)? Guided spells it out; Blind makes you call it. Goal: five in a row.

Streak 0  ·  Best 0
Still in parallel — last stretch. Keep finishing any slot the old stage-2/3 way if it fights you; you lose nothing. But with evicting added, you can now build any slot deliberately: free the edge, pair, insert. When all four slots feel reliable end-to-end, tell me — that's when we retire stages 2 and 3 on your solve card and fold F2L in for good. One case still waits for the very end: when the corner and edge show the same colour on top and won't pair (the "hide-and-seek" case).

Practice — this session & after

Now: trainer to five blind calls, then on the cube find a slot whose edge is stuck in the middle, rotate it to front-right, evict with R U R', and complete the pair. Do it for at least three slots across a couple of scrambles.

Between sessions: after each solve, before touching a slot say aloud "up top, or trapped?" — make the check a reflex.
